How it works

Headline to market.
One click.

Headline Odds runs silently in the background. You just browse normally.

Browse normally

Visit any supported news site. The extension scans headlines automatically — no setup, no account required.

Spot the odds pill

When a headline matches a live Kalshi market, a real-time pill appears inline — showing the current Yes price in cents.

Click to trade

Tap the pill to open a full market card with Yes/No prices, volume, and a direct link to trade on Kalshi.

FAQ

Common questions.

What is Kalshi?
Kalshi is a CFTC-regulated prediction market exchange where users trade on real-world events — Fed decisions, election outcomes, economic data, and more. Prices reflect the market's collective probability estimate, backed by real money.
Does the extension collect my data?
No. All headline matching happens locally inside your browser. No headlines, URLs, or browsing history are ever sent to our servers. The only external requests are to the Kalshi API to fetch live market data — your data is never included.
Which sites does it work on?
Headline Odds works on 28+ sites including the New York Times, BBC, Reuters, Bloomberg, CNN, Fox News, The Guardian, CNBC, Wall Street Journal, Politico, Axios, Washington Post, AP News, Google News, Yahoo News, and major crypto publications.
Is it free?
Yes, completely free to install and use. No subscription, no account required. You only need a Kalshi account if you want to place trades on a market you see.
How accurate is the headline matching?
The extension uses keyword overlap and bigram scoring to find the most relevant Kalshi markets per headline. It shows no pill rather than a false match — you'll only see odds when there's a genuinely relevant active market.
Will it slow down my browser?
No. Market data is cached locally for 5 minutes, and headline scanning is debounced so it doesn't fire on every scroll event. The performance impact is negligible on any modern device.
